Pope Pontian

Bishop of Rome from 21 July 230 to 28 September 235. During persecution under Emperor Maximinus Thrax, Pontian was exiled to Sardinia and resigned—first recorded papal abdication—to allow an orderly succession. Text adapted from Wikipedia (CC BY-SA).

Overview

Pope Pontian (Latin: Pontianus; died October 235) was the bishop of Rome from 21 July 230 to 28 September 235. In 235, under Emperor Maximinus Thrax, he was arrested and sent to Sardinia. He abdicated on 28 September 235 to enable election of a new pope, ending a long schism.

Life

Sources including Eusebius and the Liberian Catalogue describe a six-year pontificate. Under Maximinus, Pontian and the antipope Hippolytus were exiled to the Sardinian mines. Some accounts say Pontian was beaten to death soon after arrival.

Veneration

Pope Fabian returned the bodies of Pontian and Hippolytus to Rome in 236/237. Pontian was buried in the papal crypt of the Catacomb of Callixtus; the slab reads in Greek: Pontianos Episk (“Pontianus, Bishop”), with “martyr” added later.
